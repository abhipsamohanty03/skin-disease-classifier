About the Platform

Project details, methodology, core neural network concepts, and dataset details.

Classifier Methodology

DermAI showcases the application of deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) in dermatology. The underlying concept leverages a MobileNetV2 neural network structure, pretrained on millions of general images (ImageNet), and fine-tuned using transfer learning on clinical dermatological datasets. This method takes advantage of general image-feature extractors and calibrates the final decision layers to recognize specific patterns, pigmentation changes, vascular shapes, and boundary irregularities in skin lesions.

Dermatology Training Set: HAM10000

The classification engine aligns its outputs with the diagnostic categories defined in the HAM10000 dataset ("Human Against Machine with 10,000 training images"). HAM10000 is a standard benchmark collection of multi-source dermatoscopic images of common pigmented skin lesions. The 7 classes include:

1. Melanocytic Nevi (nv)
Common benign moles originating from pigment-producing melanocytes.
2. Melanoma (mel)
A highly malignant and invasive skin cancer. Requires early excision.
3. Benign Keratosis (bkl)
Non-cancerous growths such as seborrheic keratoses or solar lentigines.
4. Basal Cell Carcinoma (bcc)
Slow-growing, local destructive skin cancer that rarely spreads.
5. Actinic Keratosis (akiec)
Rough, scaly sun-damaged skin patches. Considered pre-cancerous.
6. Vascular Lesion (vasc)
Benign abnormalities of blood vessels (angiomas, pyogenic granulomas).
7. Dermatofibroma (df)
Firm, benign skin nodules typically forming after minor skin trauma.
